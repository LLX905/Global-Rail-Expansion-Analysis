# Global-Rail-Expansion-Analysis
Data-driven analysis to identify the best country for global rail expansion using passenger demand, infrastructure, economic, and tourism data.

**Overview:**

This project analyzes global railway and passenger data to identify the most suitable country for expanding an international rail business.

Using a structured, data-driven approach, the analysis evaluates market demand, infrastructure distribution, economic strength, geographic positioning, and tourism activity to support strategic decision-making.

**Business Problem**

Rail infrastructure exists across many countries, but demand, economic viability, and growth potential vary significantly.

**The key question:**
Which country offers the best opportunity for rail expansion?



The analysis follows a step-by-step filtering framework:

**1. Market Demand**
- Top 10 countries account for 91% of global passenger travel
- Demand is highly concentrated

**2. Infrastructure Analysis**
- Top 10 countries hold 60.1% of total rail lines
- Indicates imbalance between demand and supply

**3. Economic Filtering**
- Countries evaluated using:
- GDP per capita
- Ease of doing business
Reduced from 10 → 6 high-potential markets

**4. Geographic Analysis**
- Identified countries with strong cross-border connectivity
Enables scalable expansion opportunities

**5. Market Attractiveness**
- Tourism used as a demand indicator
-- France attracts ~5.5× more tourists than Germany



**Final Recommendation:**

**France** emerges as the optimal market due to:
- Strong passenger demand
- Well-developed rail infrastructure
- High tourism inflow
- Favorable economic environment
- Strategic geographic location
